# Design Shortcomings and New Direction

## Problem With Current Generated Design

The current generated homepage has good structure but weak visual execution.

Issues:
- Too pale and low contrast.
- Looks like an AI-generated SaaS layout.
- CTA colors do not align with Francis logo.
- Greenish buttons feel off-brand.
- Not enough deep navy / medical blue.
- Image placeholders feel unfinished.
- Hero does not feel emotionally strong.
- Doctor proof is not visually strong enough.
- Exotic pet authority is present in copy but not visually dominant enough.
- Reviews are useful but not given enough premium treatment.
- Sections feel flat and repetitive.
- It does not visually compete with the Angel Pet Hospital reference design.

## New Design Direction

The new design should feel:
- premium
- medical
- local
- warm
- trustworthy
- clean
- calm
- conversion-focused
- exotic-pet capable

It should not feel:
- like a generic veterinary template
- like a SaaS landing page
- like a pet shop
- like a coupon business
- like a playful daycare brand

## Brand System

Use the Francis Petcare logo as visual anchor.

Primary visual palette:
- deep navy / medical blue
- white
- soft blue-gray
- controlled teal/aqua for exotic-care accents
- subtle warm cream/gold accents

Do not use random green CTA buttons.

## Design References

Use Webflow veterinary template homepage as inspiration for:

## Homepage Redesign Requirements

Hero:
- split layout
- left: Richmond animal hospital + exotic pet positioning
- right: premium image collage/card system
- floating rabbit/exotic review card
- small location/hours card
- strong navy/blue CTAs

Above fold must communicate:
- Francis Petcare Hospital is in Richmond, BC
- it treats cats and dogs
- it treats rabbits, reptiles, ferrets, guinea pigs, hamsters and hedgehogs
- same-day/urgent appointments may be available during clinic hours
- visitors can call or book easily

Homepage sections:
1. Hero
2. Trust/quick facts strip
3. Everyday care + exotic care service overview
4. Signature exotic pet care section with Dr. Martinez proof
5. Core services cards
6. Doctor proof section
7. Recent Google review section
8. Urgent care / clear plan section
9. Appointment/contact section
10. FAQ section
11. Final CTA
12. Footer
